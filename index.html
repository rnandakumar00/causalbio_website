The problem
01

PD-efficacy disconnects

Target engagement is confirmed. Downstream biology isn't responding. Standard modeling does not explain why, and can't predict which patients will respond.

02

Resistance mechanisms missed early

PTEN loss, RAS amplification, feedback reactivation. Resistance is pathway-level. Correlation-based models can't capture the causal structure driving it.

03

Combination failures

Two drugs with strong single-agent data fail in combination. The interaction lives in the signaling network, not in either drug's monotherapy profile.

04

Late-stage surprises

Phase 2 failures that look like bad luck often have a mechanistic explanation. The signal was in the biology all along. It just was not modeled causally.

How it works

Mechanistic models.
Not black boxes.

Causal Bio builds ODE-based pathway models calibrated to your program's biology. Every prediction has a mechanistic explanation: no correlation, no statistical inference from population data.

01

Program review

We review your trial data, biomarker readouts, and clinical context. We identify which failure modes are mechanistically plausible for your compound and indication.

02

Model calibration

Pathway models are calibrated against an extensive set of literature-derived constraints spanning multiple biological contexts. The model captures the causal structure of the signaling network relevant to your compound and indication.

03

Retrospective analysis

We run simulations across the relevant genotypic and phenotypic contexts. We identify where the biology diverges from the drug's intended mechanism.

04

Prospective prediction

For active programs, we run forward predictions: resistance emergence, combination response, and patient stratification hypotheses with mechanistic justification.

About

Built by a computational biologist, not a software team.

Causal Bio was founded by Rohit Nandakumar, a computational biologist trained at Carnegie Mellon University. The platform was built from the ground up: pathway topology, ODE architecture, calibration pipeline, and constraint validation as a unified system for oncology drug response modeling.

The approach is deliberately mechanistic. We model signaling networks as dynamic systems, not as static interaction databases. Predictions are explainable because the causal structure is explicit.

Causal Bio works with pharma R&D teams on programs where standard models have run out of explanatory power.
